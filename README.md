## 1. Website Overview

Ethiopian Airlines (www.ethiopianairlines.com) is the flag carrier of Ethiopia and the largest airline on the African continent. Founded in 1945, it operates flights to more than 130 international destinations and over 50 domestic routes. The airline serves millions of passengers each year and has consistently been ranked among Africa's best carriers.

For a brand of this scale, the website functions as a primary booking channel, a customer service hub, and a marketing platform. Strong SEO performance is therefore critical to reducing paid acquisition costs, growing organic bookings, and supporting the airline's global expansion goals.

> **Why SEO Matters for Ethiopian Airlines**
> - Millions of travellers search for flights, routes, and travel information on Google every day.
> - Organic search is a cost-effective channel that reduces reliance on paid advertising and OTA commissions.
> - Strong rankings for branded and route keywords protect the airline from competitor bidding on its own name.
> - Quality SEO content builds trust, authority, and brand awareness in competitive international markets.

## 4. Technical SEO Assessment

### 4.1 Page Speed

Page speed is a confirmed Google ranking factor and a critical user experience metric. A slow website increases bounce rates and reduces conversions — particularly damaging for a booking platform where users expect fast, seamless interactions.

> ⚠️ **Estimated Page Speed Issues on ethiopianairlines.com**
> - Large uncompressed images on the homepage hero banner slow initial load times.
> - Multiple third-party scripts (tracking, chat, marketing) create render-blocking delays.
> - Core Web Vitals scores (LCP, CLS) likely need improvement based on typical airline site profiles.
> - Lack of next-gen image formats (WebP) adds unnecessary file size overhead.
> - **Recommendation:** Target a Google PageSpeed Insights score of 80+ on mobile and 90+ on desktop.

### 4.3 Structured Data Recommendations

Implementing structured data (Schema.org markup) enables Google to display rich results — such as flight prices, route information, and FAQs — directly in search results, significantly improving CTR.

- Implement `FlightReservation` and `AirlineReservation` schema on booking confirmation pages.
- Add `FAQPage` schema to frequently asked questions sections (baggage, check-in, visa info).
- Use `BreadcrumbList` schema across all pages to improve site navigation in search results.
- Add `Organization` schema on the homepage with logo, social media links, and contact details.

## 5. On-Page SEO Observations

### 5.1 Content & Heading Structure

Well-structured content using proper heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3) helps both users and search engines understand the page topic. The Ethiopian Airlines homepage currently lacks a clear, keyword-rich H1 tag — this is a significant missed opportunity.

> **Recommended Heading Structure for Homepage**
> ```
> H1: Ethiopian Airlines - Africa's Largest Airline | Book Flights Online
>   H2: Popular Destinations from Addis Ababa
>   H2: Why Fly with Ethiopian Airlines?
>   H2: Our Network - 130+ Destinations Across 5 Continents
>   H2: Latest Offers and Travel Deals
>     H3: Business Class | Economy Class | Cargo Services
> ```

---

### 5.2 Internal Linking

Internal links help Google understand site structure, distribute page authority, and guide users to relevant content. The current site has reasonable internal linking but several improvements are recommended:

- Add keyword-rich anchor text to route page links (e.g., *"Addis Ababa to London flights"* instead of *"View flights"*).
- Link from the homepage to top-performing destination and route pages.
- Ensure the blog/news section links back to relevant booking pages using contextual anchors.
- Create a destinations hub page that internally links to all individual route pages.

---

### 5.3 Image Optimisation

Images are one of the most overlooked areas of on-page SEO. Missing alt texts mean search engines cannot understand image content, and oversized images directly harm page speed.

- Add descriptive alt text to all images, including the hero banner, fleet photos, and destination images.
- Use keyword-relevant file names (e.g., `ethiopian-airlines-boeing-787.jpg` rather than `IMG_4821.jpg`).
- Convert all images to WebP format for significantly smaller file sizes.
- Implement lazy loading for images below the fold to improve initial page load speed.

## 7. Practical Recommendations

The following recommendations are prioritised by impact and effort. They should be implemented as part of a structured 90-day SEO improvement plan.

---

### 🟢 1. Fix the Homepage H1 Tag
Add a single, clear H1 tag to the homepage containing the primary keyword ("Ethiopian Airlines") and a key differentiator ("Africa's Largest Airline"). This is a quick win with significant ranking impact.

> **Impact:** Very High | **Effort:** Low — 1–2 hours development

---

### 🟢 2. Optimise Meta Titles & Descriptions Site-Wide
Audit and rewrite meta titles (50–60 chars) and descriptions (150–160 chars) for all top-traffic pages including homepage, route pages, and booking pages. Include primary keywords and clear calls-to-action.

> **Impact:** High | **Effort:** Medium — 1–2 weeks for full site

---

### 🟢 3. Implement Structured Data (Schema Markup)
Add `FAQPage` schema to FAQ sections, `Organization` schema to the homepage, and `BreadcrumbList` schema across all pages. This improves CTR through rich snippets and helps Google understand site structure.

> **Impact:** High | **Effort:** Medium — requires developer support

---

### 🟢 4. Improve Page Speed — Especially Mobile
Compress and convert hero images to WebP, defer non-critical JavaScript, enable browser caching, and use a CDN. Target a Google PageSpeed score of 80+ on mobile to satisfy Core Web Vitals requirements.

> **Impact:** Very High | **Effort:** High — requires technical team involvement

---

### 🟢 5. Create and Optimise Route-Specific Landing Pages
Build dedicated, keyword-optimised landing pages for top routes (e.g., "Addis Ababa to London", "Addis Ababa to Dubai"). Each page should target long-tail route keywords with unique content, FAQs, and booking CTAs.

> **Impact:** Very High | **Effort:** High — content creation required

---

### 🟢 6. Fix Image Alt Texts Across the Site
Audit all images and add descriptive, keyword-relevant alt text. This improves image search rankings, supports accessibility (WCAG compliance), and sends additional relevance signals to Google.

> **Impact:** Medium | **Effort:** Low–Medium — 1 week for audit and fixes

---

### 🟢 7. Launch an SEO Content Strategy (Blog / Travel Hub)
Publish regular content targeting informational keywords: destination guides, travel tips, route highlights, and visa information. This builds topical authority and attracts organic links from travel publications.

> **Impact:** High | **Effort:** High — ongoing commitment required

---

### 🟢 8. Audit and Fix Hreflang Implementation
Ensure all language/regional variants of pages have correct hreflang tags (e.g., `en-US`, `fr-FR`, `am-ET`). This prevents duplicate content issues across language versions and ensures correct geographic targeting in search.

> **Impact:** Medium | **Effort:** Medium — requires technical audit

## 10. Conclusion

Ethiopian Airlines has a strong brand, an extensive global network, and a natural authority advantage in African aviation search. However, the website is currently leaving significant organic search value on the table due to missing foundational SEO elements — particularly the absence of a keyword-rich H1 tag, incomplete structured data, suboptimal page speed on mobile, and inconsistently optimised meta tags.

The good news is that many of the highest-impact improvements are technically straightforward and can be implemented quickly. A focused 90-day SEO effort targeting the eight recommendations in this report could realistically deliver a **20–30% increase in organic traffic**, improved Core Web Vitals scores, and stronger rankings for high-value transactional keywords in key markets.

SEO is not a one-time task but an ongoing discipline. Regular audits, fresh content, and consistent monitoring using Google Search Console and Analytics will ensure the airline maintains and grows its organic search performance over time.
